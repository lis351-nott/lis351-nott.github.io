COMP SCI 525 - Linear Optimization

You are an owner of a restaurant. Today, as usual, there are an overwhelming number of customers storming in. With this much royal customers, you may assume that any dish that you cook will be purchased. Because of this, a day always ends with your materials run out, thus cannot cook anymore, leaving some customers hanging their heads and going home without being able to eat your delicious food.

You can formulate this wonderful situation into the following:

Ingredients
There are M types of ingredients in your restaurant.
At the start of today, you have b[j] kilograms of j-th type of ingredients. (for all 1 <= j <= M)
Menu
There are N menus in your restaurant.
The i-th menu uses a[i][j] kilograms of j-th type of ingredients. (for all 1 <= i <= N, 1 <= j <= M)
The i-th menu has a revenue of c[i] dollars per dish

Question: How much of each menu should you produce so that you have the maximum revenue possible?

So in this class, we study the formulation of various problems, starting from problems in your everyday life to some complex city planning problems, into a linear program.

We will also learn the "efficient" algorithms to solve these problems. In particular, we will learn how to develop the famous Simplex Method from ground up.

By the end of the course, you will have a very deep understanding of how to solve linear programming problem efficiently, and probably have a better understanding of what you have been using in GAMS to solve linear programming problems. (LOL)

Unfortunately, there is no programming in this course. This is a very linear algebra heavy course. You will be doing a lot of proofs, so be prepared. (Though I would say they are not too strict on the writing.)

Overall, I really enjoy this course! If you are interested in this course, OR you have enrolled this course and want to learn more about optimization, consider the following courses,

  • ISyE/Math/CS 425: Introduction to Combinatorial Optimization
  • CS/ECE/ISyE 524: Introduction to Optimization
  • CS/ISyE 526: Advanced Linear Programming
  • ISyE/Math/CS/Stat 726: Nonlinear Optimization I
  • ISyE/Math/CS 728: Integer Optimization
