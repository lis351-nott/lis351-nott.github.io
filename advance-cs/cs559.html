COMP SCI 559 - Computer Graphics

Have you watched Frozen (2013)?

Are you following any Vtubers right now?

Ever since when I was a kid, I have watched a lot of computer-animated films, including Monster Inc (2001), Toy Story (1995, 1999, 2010, 2019), Finding Nemo (2003), and Kung Fu Panda (2018).

I was fascinated by those movies, and become even more interested after I learn how to program, even more fascinated after the appearance of Vtubers.

"So, are you saying this course is about the history and how to create those graphics?". Well, yes.

"Oh! Does that mean we are going learn how to use Blender or Maya?" No. We will go beyond that. We are not going to learn how to use Blender to create graphics nor animations, but we are going to learn how Blender are written.

Okay, that was a bit exaggerated, but I hope you get the idea. In this course, we are going to learn how to communicate with computer hardware to "draw" things on the screen for us.

You will have a very sharp granularity in controlling what will be drawn on the screen, even down to a single pixel.

However, you need to learn how to "orchestrate" what and where to appears on the screen so that it matches you have imagined.

Learning how to "orchestrate" include, but not limited to,

  • How to represent "points", "lines" or "shapes"?
  • What does it mean to "rotate", "scale" and "transform"?
  • How do you define "curves"? What are the advantages and drawbacks for each techniques?
  • lots of linear algebra
  • How do you all of the above in three dimensions?
  • How do you "paint" the surface of an object? How do you use that "painting" to create an illusion that a smooth object has bumps?

By the end of the course, you will gain an insight on the low-level of how computer-animated graphics and Vtubers are created. At that point, you can study more about graphics on your own.

This is my first project on 3D graphics on the course. Written purely in vanilla JavaScript

We will be using JavaScript for the communication in this course. The background of JavaScript is not required prior to the course.

Do be prepared to write a lot of code. This is a programming course for a reason.
